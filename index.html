Welcome passengers

Discover the most used aircraft around the world

Airbus - A320

As the undisputed leader in the single-aisle category, Airbus’ A320 is the aircraft family of choice for airlines around the world – from low-cost operators to mainline carriers. (Turbofan twin-engine narrow-body aircraft)

Boeing 737-800

The 737-800 can seat 162 passengers in a 2-class configuration, or 189 in a single-class configuration. It has a range of 5 670 km (3060 nautical miles) and is equipped with CFMI CFM 56-7 engines.

ATR 72-600

The ATR-72 is a twin-engine, pressurized, medium-sized, turboprop, high-wing commercial aircraft. It has the capacity to carry up to 72 passengers on regional flights. (ATR - Regional Transport Planes).

Airbus - A321

The A321’s cabin offers passengers even more space in which to relax and enjoy the flight. Able to comfortably seat anywhere from a typical 170 to 200 passengers in a standard two-class cabin to 220 travellers in a more high-density layout.

Embraer E195 - E2

A combination of capacity and range in one high-performance aircraft, the E195-E2 is the ideal tool for operators wanting to enter new markets and take advantage of new opportunities. The E195-E2's range extends to 2,600nm (4,815km).

Airbus A330-neo

As the A330neo Family’s largest member, the A330-900 retains the fuselage length of Airbus’ A330-300 variant to accommodate between 260 and 300 seats in a typical three-class layout, or up to 460 in economy class.

Boeing 747-800

It is a high-capacity aircraft from Boeing, which offers airlines the lowest operating costs and the greatest economic advantages of all passenger and cargo aircraft in its class - while still offering better environmental performance.

Airbus A350-900

In providing ultimate flexibility and versatility to operators, the uncompromised all-new design of the A350-900 delivers capacity, range, and economics, while providing the best cabin environment. (Shaping the future of air travel).

Airbus - A380

Boarding an A380 is a unique experience that introduces passengers to superior standards of in-flight comfort, from first class to economy. (This is the largest passenger aircraft in the world developed by Airbus).
